# Arachine Swamp

Arachine Swamp one of the least traveled area in Abryryim. Weather in the Arachine Swamp was unnatural and inhospitable, and made it a place of unending discomfort. Rain fell regularly, regardless of the season, seemingly falling whenever it would be most annoying. The annual total precipitation was 70 inches (1.78 meters). In summer and autumn, temperatures were oppressively hot (thanks to the humidity), with peak temperatures of 90s while in winter and spring they were relatively cold, dipping as low as 40s. It never saw snow.Whether in the depths of winter or the height of summer, and even in bright sun, the marsh and forest were forever shrouded with a persistent mist or murky vapor. Thin and sticky, it hugged the branches of the trees, pooled in depressions and hollows, and flowed over land and around obstacles, moving almost like a living thing. The ever-present and obscuring mist made it hard to see more than a mile in any direction, even if one could see over or past the trees.

# Cillicia
Cillicia is home to 22,000 souls, and about the twice that number of goats, sheep, cows, as well other strange herd animals. The climate promotes agriculture and vast grazing fields around the city are managed by hundreds of herders. Producing most of the meat and livestock for the cities south and east of the Zephyr Mountains the city is positioned well as a neutral state open to all who would promote the free trade. The city is actually made of many loose-fitting villages in close proximity, which representatives provide votes on matters important to the city commonwealth. # Ebonhaven
Nestled between rugged mountain ranges and the shores of a crystal-clear spring-fed lake, Ebonhaven is a thriving city of nearly 16,000 residents. Despite its prosperity, the region receives very little rainfall due to its location within a rain shadow. The surrounding hills and mountains are rich in silver, iron, and other valuable ores, making mining the cornerstone of Ebonhaven's economy. Caravans regularly arrive to purchase the city's metals, tools, and crafted goods, bringing wealth and influence to this otherwise isolated settlement.

##### Government
Ebonhaven is governed by a unique religious council known as The Ruling Five. Unlike hereditary monarchies or elected councils, political power in the city is determined by faith and influence.Each year, all recognized churches within Ebonhaven conduct a census of their congregations. The five churches with the largest number of followers earn a seat on the council for the following year. Each church leader receives one vote, and all major civic decisions are decided by majority rule.This system creates constant competition among the city's faiths. Churches actively recruit new followers, sponsor public works, and seek to increase their influence among the populace. Alliances and rivalries between religious factions shape much of Ebonhaven's politics.
##### The Walkers
One of Ebonhaven's most famous traditions revolves around a group known as the Walkers.

For generations, explorers, adventurers, merchants, and dreamers have searched for a secure route through the mountains leading to the distant city of Windmire. Numerous trails have been discovered, but none are safe or reliable enough to support regular trade caravans or pack animals.

Those who dedicate themselves to this search are called Walkers. Every year new expeditions venture into the peaks, hoping to succeed where countless others have failed.

The reward would be immense. Anyone who discovers and maps a route stable enough for mule caravans to travel safely would earn a fortune beyond imagining. Such a passage would transform regional trade and secure the discoverer's place in Ebonhaven's history forever.

Notable Features
Population: Approximately 16,000.
Government: The Ruling Five, composed of the leaders of the five largest churches.
Economy: Mining, metalworking, and trade.
Water Source: A massive spring-fed lake that provides fresh water year-round.
Climate: Dry and arid with very little annual rainfall.
Local Legend: The endless search for a trade route through the mountains to Windmire.
Political Climate: Religious competition is fierce as churches vie for membership and a seat among the Ruling Five.

# Elisheba

The oldest city in all of Abryryim, Elisheba is home to 85,000 souls.  Many scholars believe that all life on Abryryim originated from the mires surrounding Elisheba, and the remnants of ancient cultures are often found washed up on the tumultuous banks of the bog.  The city is ruled by a council of eight leaders that promote an ancient superstition regarding the power of the number eight.  The octagonal stone walls of the city strictly divide it into eight sectors, with eight districts containing eight blocks of eight buildings.  New construction is necessarily always vertical which has resulted in the city and society being divided into three tiers (though the council of eight is in the process of designing the remaining five tiers).  The rich and powerful rule from atop the city in Tier Three and rarely ever see, let alone touch the filthy Abryryim earth beneath them.  Well-guarded and maintained ramps have been constructed to allow T3s to bypass all of T2 and T1 to enter and exit the city, and their complete grip on industry has created incredible economic disparity.  Those in Tier Two are the physical laborers of the city and provide nearly all vital services to the economy and health of Elisheba.  Without dedicated ramps out of the city, the lack of quick access to sunlight has had significant impacts on those living here. The dregs of society scrape away a meager life in the slums of tier one Elisheba.  Responsible for the sewer systems, trash collection and disposal, as well as rearing all of the animals of Elisheba, T3s are a vital source of labor for the Council of Eight.  The Council's eight visits per year are celebrated as donations are always made to high-performing sectors, along with promises of a better life someday.

# Old Light

Old Light: a smaller island city of 4,000 souls, the town was called "Blue Star" until the beginning of the 3rd age when a massive sea dragon summoned a tsunami that destroyed most of the town. The previous inhabitants of Old Light made a very lucrative business of hunting local sea dragons and selling off their meat, scales, fangs, and horns. One group of sailors captured what turned out to be the child of an ancient sea dragon that took revenge on the fishermen and their town. Today the occupants focus on fishing for smaller aquatic game, harvesting pearls and other rare coral from the local waters, and boasts a massive jewelry industry. Old Light is known for welcoming all races, cultures, and beliefs, so the population is very diverse. A council of 12 elected officials loosely governs the city, hearing cases brought before them and handing down judgements that are then carried out by the common folk. This creates an atmosphere of near self-government with citizens living their daily lives on mostly an honor system and solidifies a symbiotic relationship between all of the citizens. Old Light is whispered to be a haven for pirates, the most skilled of which can supposedly evade capture by sailing through the dangerous Misty Moores. The numerous brothels and taverns on the island may be seen as corroborating these claims.
