Brokkur "Gearsolder" Stonehelm
Class: Artificer (Armorer subclass)
Homeland: Windford, the City of Vaults

---

### Act I: The Ticking Cradle
To the dwarves of Windford, stone is sacred. They shape it, mine it, and worship its silence. But from the moment Brokkur was born, he did not care for the quiet of stone. He cared for the clack-clack-clack of brass.

His mother, Astrid, often tells the story of how she would lay him down on a soft woolen blanket, only to find him minutes later, crawling with terrifying speed toward the nearest heat-lamp or ticking clockwork surveyor. He didn't play with wooden toys; he played with the gears his father brought home from the deep vaults.

By the age of eight, Brokkur was the terror of the Windford lower districts. If a pneumatic elevator stopped working, or a mechanical oyster-crusher went silent, everyone knew to look for the boy. He would get into trouble constantly—sneaking into locked guild-houses, disassembling expensive steam-valves, and leaving the gears scattered across the floor. He would get yelled at, his ears ringing from his mother’s scoldings and the guild-masters' wrath. But when they forced him to put the machines back together, they would find something baffling: the elevator ran smoother, the valves handled twice the pressure, and the oyster-crusher no longer jammed.

He didn't just understand machines. He spoke to them. And they spoke back.

### Act II: The Protocol of Lord Grizfane
His father, Thrain, was a surveyor for Lord Grizfane Goldentooth, tasked with mapping the deepest, most dangerous vaults carved into Windford’s coastal cliffs. Thrain was Brokkur’s hero—a quiet dwarf who wore the scent of sea-salt and coal smoke, and who always brought Brokkur strange, broken mechanical scrap from the deep dark.

When Brokkur was twelve, his father took him deep into the vaults to show him the great pneumatic water-gates. It was a routine inspection. But deep beneath the ocean level, a sudden seismic tremor tore through the cliffside. A support beam snapped. A massive stone block crashed down, pinning Thrain’s leg to the stone floor.

At the same moment, a high-pressure sea-pipe ruptured. Freezing ocean water began to flood the chamber.

Because Windford’s wealth is concentrated in these vaults, Lord Grizfane’s security protocols are absolute, cold, and automated: If a breach occurs, the vaults seal and flood to prevent loss of treasure or unauthorized entry.

The massive, mithral-reinforced emergency vault door began its slow, grinding, pneumatic descent. Brokkur, too small and weak to lift the stone off his father, ran to the manual override lever on the wall. He threw his entire weight onto it.

But he was just a child. His muscles weren't developed enough to throw the heavy iron clutch. The gears locked. He screamed, his tiny hands slipping on the cold iron.

His father, water rising to his chest, looked at Brokkur through the narrowing gap of the descending door. “Go, Brokkur! Run!”

Brokkur didn't run. He hammered on the override panel with a wrench, trying to bypass the lock, trying to understand the runes, crying as the freezing water splashed his face. The massive door ground shut with a deafening thud, locking his father inside. Through the thick, reinforced viewing pane, Brokkur watched the chamber fill to the ceiling.

He was entirely helpless. He had all the talent in the world, and it hadn't saved the only man who truly believed in him.

### Act III: "Never Out of Reach"
The grief didn't break Brokkur; it forged him. He swore a solemn oath to his father’s memory: No one would ever die because a machine failed, or because he was too weak to reach them.

He stopped building weapons of war. He dedicated his genius to the art of preservation.

* The Aegis Dome: If he’d had a force field, he could have held back the collapsing ceiling and the rushing water. He built a portable, hexagonal projector that hums to life, casting a shimmering barrier of kinetic energy to keep his allies safe.
* The Stun Grenade: He created flashbangs and electromagnetic disruptors. He realized that violence only causes structural damage—it collapses tunnels and triggers cave-ins. It is far better to disorient and disable a threat instantly, keeping the environment safe for his friends.
* Biotic Bullets (The Injector-Rifle): He built a custom pneumatic rifle, but it doesn't fire lead. It fires pressurized, alchemical darts. When his allies are wounded or pinned down on the other side of a battlefield—where he cannot physically reach them—he chambers a capsule of glowing, life-saving fluid and shoots them. The needle dissolves instantly, knitting their wounds and surging them with adrenaline. He can heal from a distance, ensuring no one is ever "behind the vault door" again.

### The Current Quest: Seeking the Forge of Heaven
Brokkur has left Windford. He is traveling the world of Abryryim as an adventurer, testing his defensive gadgets in the harshest environments. But his ultimate goal lies back in his homeland.

He wants to return to Windford, bypass Lord Grizfane's "unbreachable" vault defenses, and recover his father’s remains. To do that, he needs to build a device capable of withstand the crushing pressure of the flooded vaults. He needs Dark Diamond Ore, and he needs to learn the secrets of the Forge of Heaven to shape it into the ultimate protective suit.

He travels with your group not just as a comrade, but as their guardian angel. He watches over them with a hyper-vigilant, protective eye, constantly tinkering, constantly upgrading, ensuring that this time, everyone comes home alive.
